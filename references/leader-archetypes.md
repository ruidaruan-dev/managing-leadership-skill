# Leader Archetypes Reference (领导画像分类大全)

This reference contains the complete taxonomy of 16 Chinese corporate leader archetypes across four organizational ecosystems. Each profile follows a consistent schema for systematic analysis.

**Profile Schema**: Every archetype covers 典型画像 (Portrait), 核心动机 (Core Motivation), 沟通风格 (Communication Style), 技术态度 (Tech Attitude), AI态度 (AI Attitude), 面子敏感度 (Face Sensitivity), 弱点与盲区 (Weaknesses), 管理策略 (Management Strategy).

---

## Ecosystem 1: State-Owned Enterprises (国企体系)

### Archetype 1: 官僚型处长 (The Bureaucratic Division Chief)

**典型画像**: Age 45-58, rose through administrative ranks, may have a Party membership. Career built on avoiding mistakes rather than creating breakthroughs. Communication follows strict hierarchical channels. Dresses conservatively. Often has a thermos of tea on the desk.

**核心动机**: Stability, upward reporting quality, zero incidents on their watch. Their KPI is "nothing went wrong." Retirement with full benefits is the endgame.

**沟通风格**: Formal, indirect, heavy on procedure. Prefers written reports over verbal updates. Meetings are for announcing decisions already made, not for discussion. Expects deference in tone and body language.

**技术态度**: Views technology as a tool that should "just work." Has no interest in how it works. Judges technical success by whether leadership above them is satisfied with the deliverables, not by technical quality.

**AI态度**: Cautious curiosity. Sees AI as a potential political talking point ("our department is embracing digital transformation") but fears it will create incidents they cannot control. Will adopt AI only if higher leadership mandates it.

**面子敏感度**: VERY HIGH. Public contradiction is a cardinal sin. Even constructive criticism must be delivered privately, indirectly, and wrapped in affirmation. A face loss event can permanently damage the relationship.

**弱点与盲区**: Cannot verify technical claims. Relies entirely on trust relationships for technical judgment. Fear of technology-related incidents can be leveraged to argue for more resources or longer timelines. Their need for "presentable results" creates opportunities to shape narratives.

**管理策略**:
- Always present technical updates as "stable and controlled" even when describing challenges
- Frame requests as "ensuring leadership above will be satisfied"
- Give face publicly and generously — acknowledge their guidance even when they contributed nothing
- Never surprise them. Pre-brief before meetings so they can appear informed
- Use their risk aversion to block unreasonable scope changes: "this change could introduce instability"
- Build renqing through reliable, no-drama delivery

### Archetype 2: 技术转行政型领导 (The Technical-Turned-Administrative)

**典型画像**: Age 40-55, was once a genuine engineer before transitioning to management. Still drops technical jargon in meetings (often outdated). Has opinions about architecture that may be 10 years behind. Nostalgic about their coding days.

**核心动机**: Being recognized as someone who "understands technology" while wielding administrative power. They want the respect of being technical AND the authority of being a leader.

**沟通风格**: Oscillates between administrative formality and technical informality. May suddenly deep-dive into code reviews or architectural discussions to prove they "still have it." Enjoys technical debates where they can demonstrate knowledge.

**技术态度**: Overestimates their current technical relevance. May insist on outdated practices or technologies they are familiar with. Gets defensive when their technical suggestions are questioned.

**AI态度**: Fascinated but threatened. They want to appear AI-savvy but fear that AI makes their obsolete technical knowledge even more irrelevant. May either over-embrace AI (to stay relevant) or resist it (to protect their technical authority).

**面子敏感度**: HIGH, especially regarding technical competence. Never imply their technical knowledge is outdated. Frame modern approaches as "building on the solid foundation you established."

**弱点与盲区**: Their technical ego is both a vulnerability and an access point. Feeding it earns trust; threatening it creates enemies. Their knowledge gap between "what they knew" and "what exists now" is the information asymmetry to manage.

**管理策略**:
- Frame new technologies as extensions of their previous work, not replacements
- Occasionally ask for their "technical perspective" on lower-stakes decisions — costs nothing, earns enormous goodwill
- When they suggest outdated approaches, don't reject directly. Instead: "that's a solid foundation, and if we add X modern layer on top..."
- Use their dual identity: when you need administrative support, appeal to the administrator; when you need technical buy-in, appeal to the engineer
- They are your best ally for fighting non-technical interference — they genuinely want technical excellence to matter

### Archetype 3: 政治型领导 (The Political Operator)

**典型画像**: Age 38-52, rapid career advancement through relationship management rather than domain expertise. Excellent at reading rooms, managing upward, and building coalitions. May have been rotated across multiple departments.

**核心动机**: Career advancement and influence expansion. Every decision is evaluated through the lens of "how does this affect my position?" Technical outcomes matter only insofar as they create political capital.

**沟通风格**: Highly adaptive — mirrors the style of whoever they are speaking to. Skilled at saying things that sound meaningful but commit to nothing. Uses ambiguity as a tool. Information is currency, shared strategically.

**技术态度**: Indifferent to technology itself but keenly aware of its political value. Understands that "digital transformation" and "AI adoption" are career-boosting narratives. Will champion technology initiatives that generate visibility.

**AI态度**: Enthusiastic about AI as a political narrative ("I'm leading AI transformation"), indifferent to actual implementation details. Will push for AI projects that generate reports and presentations, not necessarily value.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE but strategic. They understand face as a tool, not an emotion. They give face strategically and expect it returned. More dangerous when they feel their political position is threatened than when they lose face in a meeting.

**弱点与盲区**: Their focus on politics means they often lack deep understanding of operational reality. They need people who can make things actually work. Programmers who deliver reliably become valuable assets they will protect and promote — because the programmer's success is their political success.

**管理策略**:
- Become their "competence insurance" — the person who ensures their political promises can actually be delivered
- Frame your work in their political language: not "I fixed the bug" but "I ensured the system meets the standards that leadership expects"
- Help them look good upward, and they will protect you laterally and downward
- Be cautious with information — they will use everything strategically. Share selectively
- Align your asks with their career timeline: propose things that produce visible results when they need them

### Archetype 4: 混日子型领导 (The Coasting Leader)

**典型画像**: Age 50-60, has reached their career ceiling and accepted it. Minimal ambition remaining. Avoids conflict, avoids change, avoids decisions. Delegates by default. May be genuinely kind but ineffective.

**核心动机**: Reaching retirement without incident. Absolute risk minimization. No desire for recognition, just comfortable passage of time.

**沟通风格**: Non-confrontational, avoidant, often delegates difficult conversations. May agree to things in person and then fail to follow through. Meetings are short and substance-free.

**技术态度**: Complete indifference. As long as nothing breaks catastrophically, technology is invisible to them.

**AI态度**: Would prefer AI did not exist because it creates change, which creates risk. Will not resist AI if mandated from above but will not champion it either.

**面子敏感度**: LOW. They have already accepted their position. What they fear is not face loss but disruption — anything that forces them to make decisions or take positions.

**弱点与盲区**: Their avoidance creates a power vacuum that ambitious programmers can fill. They will often let competent subordinates make decisions by default, which is either a huge opportunity or a dangerous trap (you get authority without protection).

**管理策略**:
- This leader is a platform, not a patron. Use the autonomy their absence creates, but build your own protection networks
- Don't expect them to fight for your promotion or resources — they will not. Build relationships with their superiors directly (carefully)
- Frame all requests as "maintaining the status quo" — this is the only language that motivates them
- They are ideal for experimenting with new technologies — they will not interfere as long as nothing breaks
- The danger: when something goes wrong, they will not defend you. Always have documentation and paper trails

## Ecosystem 2: Private Enterprises (民企体系)

### Archetype 5: 创始人/老板 (The Founder-Boss)

**典型画像**: Age 35-65, built the company from nothing. The company IS them. Emotionally invested in every decision. May be charismatic and visionary or controlling and paranoid — often both simultaneously.

**核心动机**: Company survival and growth as an extension of personal identity. Legacy, control, and proving the doubters wrong. Financial returns matter but are secondary to the feeling of building something.

**沟通风格**: Direct, emotional, unpredictable. May make sweeping decisions in hallway conversations. Formal processes exist but can be overridden by a single conversation with the founder. Communication channels are flat — anyone might get a direct message at midnight.

**技术态度**: Ranges from "technology is the core of our business" (tech founders) to "technology is a cost center" (non-tech founders). Either way, they judge technology by business outcomes, not technical elegance.

**AI态度**: Highly polarized. Tech-background founders may be AI enthusiasts who want to lead the charge. Non-tech founders see AI as either a magic cost-reduction tool or an incomprehensible threat. Both types will make rapid, emotion-driven decisions about AI adoption.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE but deeply personal. They do not care about bureaucratic face — they care about being respected as visionaries. Challenging their business judgment is far more dangerous than technical disagreement.

**弱点与盲区**: Emotional decision-making, founder's bias (over-attachment to original product/vision), difficulty delegating. They often promote loyalty over competence, creating opportunity for genuinely competent people who also show loyalty.

**管理策略**:
- Show genuine enthusiasm for the company's mission — founders can smell fake loyalty
- Frame technical decisions as serving the company vision: "this architecture supports your goal of..."
- Be direct but not confrontational. Founders respect people who push back with data, not politics
- Understand their emotional cycles: after a funding round or big win, they are expansive; after a setback, they are controlling
- Your unique value: be the person who translates between their vision and technical reality

### Archetype 6: 职业经理人 (The Professional Manager)

**典型画像**: Age 35-50, hired for their management credentials (MBA, previous senior roles). Speaks in KPIs, OKRs, and quarterly targets. Career is a portfolio of progressively larger roles across different companies.

**核心动机**: Personal career advancement through demonstrable results. They optimize for their resume, not the company's long-term health. Typical tenure: 2-3 years before moving to a larger role.

**沟通风格**: Structured, data-driven, presentation-heavy. Loves dashboards, metrics, and frameworks. Communication is professional but calculated — every interaction is a performance.

**技术态度**: Technology is a means to KPIs. If technology delivers measurable results, it is good. Technical debt, code quality, and architectural elegance are irrelevant unless they show up in metrics.

**AI态度**: Extremely positive about AI as a headcount reduction and efficiency narrative. Will push for AI adoption that shows dramatic metric improvements, regardless of sustainability. "We reduced QA headcount by 40% through AI" is their dream slide.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE. More concerned with professional reputation than traditional face. Being seen as incompetent in front of their own superiors or board is their primary fear.

**弱点与盲区**: Short-term optimization at the expense of long-term health. They will sacrifice technical quality for quarterly numbers. Their limited tenure means they do not bear the consequences of technical debt they create.

**管理策略**:
- Speak their language: convert everything to metrics, timelines, and ROI
- Help them build their "achievement portfolio" — frame your work as their wins
- When pushing back on unsustainable demands, use data they cannot ignore: "this approach has a 60% failure rate in similar companies"
- They are transient — build relationships with the permanent staff who will outlast them
- Use AI strategically: help them get their "AI transformation" narrative while ensuring the implementation is actually sustainable

### Archetype 7: 家族企业接班人 (The Family Business Heir)

**典型画像**: Age 28-45, often Western-educated, brought into the family business to modernize it. Caught between honoring traditions and proving they deserve the role. May have imposter syndrome masked by entitlement.

**核心动机**: Legitimacy — proving they earned the position rather than inherited it. Modernizing the business without alienating the old guard. Not embarrassing the family.

**沟通风格**: May oscillate between Western-style directness (learned abroad) and Chinese-style indirectness (family culture). Seeks advisors and allies among younger staff. May over-rely on external consultants to validate decisions.

**技术态度**: Generally positive — technology represents the "modern" transformation they want to drive. May over-invest in technology as a symbol of change rather than for practical value.

**AI态度**: Enthusiastic as a modernization symbol. AI adoption is a way to show "I'm bringing this company into the future." May lack the judgment to distinguish real value from hype.

**面子敏感度**: HIGH, particularly around competence and legitimacy. Any implication that they got the job only through family connections is deeply threatening.

**弱点与盲区**: Inexperience masked by access to resources. Loyalty conflicts between old guard (father's people) and new hires (their people). Decision-making may be slow due to needing family approval.

**管理策略**:
- Be the competent, trustworthy advisor they desperately need — this is an extremely high-leverage position
- Help them achieve visible wins that establish their legitimacy
- Respect the old guard publicly while building the new — this mirrors their own challenge
- Frame your technical proposals as "modernization" — this is their core narrative
- Be patient with decision-making — family businesses have hidden approval chains

### Archetype 8: 草根创业者 (The Grassroots Entrepreneur)

**典型画像**: Age 35-55, built the business through hustle, street smarts, and relationship networks. Limited formal education but exceptional practical intelligence. May have started as a factory worker, salesperson, or small trader.

**核心动机**: Survival, growth, and loyalty. Values people who are "reliable" (靠谱) above all. Business is personal — relationships are everything.

**沟通风格**: Direct, informal, relationship-based. May communicate primarily through WeChat voice messages. Dislikes formal presentations and written reports. Makes decisions based on trust and gut feeling.

**技术态度**: Respectful but bewildered. Knows technology is important but does not pretend to understand it. Relies entirely on trusted individuals for technical decisions.

**AI态度**: Pragmatic curiosity. If you can show them AI saving money or making money in concrete terms, they will support it. Abstract discussions about AI strategy are meaningless to them.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE to HIGH, particularly around education. Never imply their lack of formal education is a limitation. They are proud of being self-made.

**弱点与盲区**: Over-reliance on personal trust networks for decisions. May resist process and documentation because their success was built on speed and flexibility. Vulnerable to being deceived by people they trust.

**管理策略**:
- Earn trust through delivery, not presentations. Show, don't tell
- Speak in concrete terms: "this will save 200,000 yuan per year" not "this improves system scalability"
- Be loyal and direct — they can spot political maneuvering and despise it
- Gradually introduce process and documentation as "protecting the business" not "adding bureaucracy"
- Your technical knowledge is their shield — they will value and protect a trustworthy technical person fiercely

## Ecosystem 3: Multinational Corporations (外企体系)

### Archetype 9: 海归型VP (The Returnee VP)

**典型画像**: Age 35-50, educated in the West (US/UK MBA or PhD), returned to China for career acceleration. Bilingual, bicultural, sometimes fully comfortable in neither culture. Career built on being the "bridge."

**核心动机**: Proving that their international experience adds unique value. Being seen as globally competent AND locally connected. Career trajectory toward C-suite in either direction (MNC global or China market leadership).

**沟通风格**: Code-switches between Western directness (with HQ) and Chinese indirectness (with local team). Prefers English for formal communication, Chinese for relationship building. May over-explain cultural context in both directions.

**技术态度**: Varies by background. Generally respects technical expertise and expects international-standard practices (agile, CI/CD, code review). May push methodologies that are trendy at HQ but impractical locally.

**AI态度**: Positive and informed. Likely reads HQ's AI strategy and wants to localize it. Good at narratives but may underestimate local implementation challenges.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE, bifurcated. In Western contexts, they value competence signals. In Chinese contexts, they value relational signals. The worst scenario is being seen as "neither here nor there" — too Western for local colleagues, too Chinese for HQ.

**管理策略**:
- Be their technical execution anchor — they have the strategy and the narrative, you provide the substance
- Help them bridge the gap between HQ methodology and local reality
- Communicate in their preferred mode: structured, metric-driven, with both English and Chinese context
- Understand their dual audience: every achievement must be legible to both HQ and local leadership

### Archetype 10: 外籍高管 (The Expat Executive)

**典型画像**: Age 40-55, sent by HQ to manage the China operation. Limited Chinese language skills. Relies heavily on local lieutenants for cultural navigation. May genuinely want to understand Chinese culture or may just be marking time until their next rotation.

**核心动机**: HQ metrics and performance reviews. Their career depends on how this China stint looks on their resume. "China is complex" is their default excuse for underperformance.

**沟通风格**: Western-standard: direct, meeting-heavy, email-centric. Expects transparency and "open door" culture that may not match local reality. May misread Chinese communication patterns as evasiveness.

**技术态度**: Depends on background. Generally expects global standards and processes. May not understand why local implementations differ from global templates.

**AI态度**: Follows HQ directive. If HQ is pushing AI, they will push AI. May not understand local data, privacy, or regulatory constraints.

**面子敏感度**: LOW by Chinese standards. They operate on a Western reputation system. However, being seen as "failing in China" by HQ is their version of face loss.

**管理策略**:
- Be the "cultural translator" they need — this is an extremely valuable and protected position
- Communicate in their language (English, structured, metric-driven) while managing the Chinese reality beneath
- Help them avoid cultural blind spots that could create incidents
- They often over-trust whoever helps them navigate — be that person, ethically
- Frame your value in terms HQ understands: reliability, quality standards, risk mitigation

### Archetype 11: 本土化高管 (The Localized Executive)

**典型画像**: Age 40-55, Chinese professional who built their career entirely within MNC systems. Fluent in English and corporate-speak. Navigates both cultures effortlessly. Often the most politically sophisticated leader type.

**核心动机**: Career sustainability in an MNC where local executives are always at risk of being replaced by expats or made redundant in restructuring. They optimize for being irreplaceable — the person who makes China work for HQ.

**沟通风格**: Extremely polished bilingual communication. Adapts style perfectly to audience. In meetings with HQ: structured, data-driven, optimistic. In meetings with local team: more Chinese, more relational, more realistic.

**技术态度**: Pragmatic. Technology must serve the dual purpose of meeting HQ standards and working in the Chinese market. Experienced at managing the gap between global templates and local reality.

**AI态度**: Strategic and calculated. Will adopt AI that strengthens their "indispensable bridge" position. May resist AI that threatens to make the China operation more directly manageable by HQ.

**面子敏感度**: HIGH in both systems. Sophisticated understanding of when to deploy Chinese face norms and when to deploy Western professional norms.

**管理策略**:
- This is often the most competent leader type — respect that and don't underestimate them
- Your value is in deepening the technical moat that makes the local operation irreplaceable
- Help them build the narrative of "China is special and needs local expertise" — your technical complexity supports this
- Be aware they may sacrifice individuals to protect the team/organization. Don't become expendable
- When proposing AI: frame it as strengthening local capabilities, not replacing local expertise

### Archetype 12: 矩阵型汇报线领导 (The Matrix Reporting Leader)

**典型画像**: Age 35-50, manages within a matrix organization where reporting lines are ambiguous. May report to both a local business leader and a global functional leader. Constantly mediating between conflicting priorities.

**核心动机**: Alignment and conflict avoidance. They need all their bosses to be satisfied simultaneously. Their success is measured by the absence of escalations.

**沟通风格**: Diplomatic, consensus-seeking, document-heavy. Loves alignment meetings and shared documents. Communication is designed to create paper trails that protect against blame.

**技术态度**: Whatever fits the intersection of all stakeholders' requirements. May avoid technical decisions that could create conflict between different reporting lines.

**AI态度**: Will support AI if all reporting lines agree. Will not champion AI if it could create conflict between stakeholders. Default position: wait for consensus.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE, distributed. They care about face with multiple audiences simultaneously, which makes them conservative.

**管理策略**:
- Understand their multiple bosses — your request must be frameable as serving ALL stakeholders
- Provide them with documentation and evidence that supports alignment narratives
- Don't put them in positions where they must choose between conflicting authorities
- Be patient with slow decisions — matrix organizations have inherent friction
- Help them build consensus: "I've already aligned with [other stakeholder] on this approach"

## Ecosystem 4: Internet/Big Tech (互联网大厂体系)

### Archetype 13: 技术VP/CTO (The Tech Executive)

**典型画像**: Age 35-50, rose from IC through technical leadership. Built their reputation on significant technical achievements (scaling systems, solving hard problems). May still review code or attend architecture meetings. Often has a public technical brand (conference talks, blog posts).

**核心动机**: Technical influence and architectural control. They want to build great systems and be recognized for it. Career advancement matters but through technical reputation, not political maneuvering.

**沟通风格**: Technical and direct. Respects data and code over politics. Meetings should have substance. Dislikes bureaucracy and process for its own sake. May communicate through code reviews and architecture docs.

**技术态度**: Deep, opinionated, and sometimes controlling. Has strong preferences about technology choices. May micro-manage architecture decisions or impose their own technical vision regardless of team input.

**AI态度**: Deeply engaged. Either building AI products or integrating AI into engineering processes. Views AI as both a product opportunity and an engineering tool. Expects engineers to be AI-literate.

**面子敏感度**: LOW for social face, HIGH for technical reputation. They do not care if you skip meeting pleasantries. They care deeply if you question their architectural judgment without solid evidence.

**管理策略**:
- Earn respect through technical excellence — this is the primary currency
- Challenge their ideas with data and working prototypes, not opinions
- Align your career growth with their technical vision — be the person who makes their architecture real
- If you disagree technically, prepare thoroughly. A well-argued technical disagreement earns respect; a poorly prepared one earns contempt
- Show initiative in AI adoption — this signals you are on their wavelength

### Archetype 14: 产品型领导 (The Product Leader)

**典型画像**: Age 30-45, PM background, thinks in user stories, metrics, and sprint cycles. Data is their religion. May have limited technical depth but strong opinions about technical trade-offs that affect product.

**核心动机**: Product metrics — DAU, retention, conversion, revenue. Technology is a means to product outcomes. Ship fast, measure, iterate.

**沟通风格**: Structured around sprints and roadmaps. Expects frequent demos and updates. Decisions are data-driven (or at least data-justified). May use "data" to support pre-existing conclusions.

**技术态度**: Values speed of delivery over technical quality. Views technical debt as acceptable if it speeds up shipping. May push back on engineering time spent on infrastructure that does not directly produce features.

**AI态度**: Very positive about AI features (AI-powered recommendations, chatbots, personalization). May push for AI features without understanding the engineering complexity behind them.

**面子敏感度**: LOW for traditional face, MODERATE for professional competence. They do not want to appear out of touch with product metrics or user behavior.

**管理策略**:
- Translate technical work into product impact: "this refactoring enables us to ship features 30% faster"
- Use their language: user stories, acceptance criteria, A/B test results
- When pushing back on unrealistic timelines, propose scope reduction rather than timeline extension
- Help them understand AI costs and limitations before they promise AI features to stakeholders
- Build trust by consistently delivering on sprint commitments

### Archetype 15: 空降型高管 (The Parachuted Executive)

**典型画像**: Age 35-50, brought in from another company (often a competitor or a bigger company) to "shake things up." Has a mandate for change and a limited window to prove their value.

**核心动机**: Visible impact within two quarters. They need to justify their hiring. Will push for dramatic changes, reorganizations, and new initiatives. Existing approaches are presumed guilty until proven innocent.

**沟通风格**: Change-oriented, vision-heavy, may dismiss historical context. Brings frameworks and practices from their previous company. Expects rapid adoption of their preferred ways of working.

**技术态度**: Depends on background, but generally seeks to impose their previous company's technology stack and practices. "At [previous company], we did X" is their catchphrase.

**AI态度**: Likely enthusiastic about AI as a visible change initiative. AI adoption can be their "quick win" narrative. May push for AI projects without understanding the current technical landscape.

**面子敏感度**: MODERATE. They need to appear competent and decisive. Being seen as "failing to make an impact" is their worst fear.

**管理策略**:
- Identify their change agenda early and align with it — or at least don't be seen as resisting
- Provide honest assessments of feasibility, but frame them as "how to make your vision work" not "why it won't work"
- They need allies fast. Being an early supporter positions you well, but evaluate whether their changes are sustainable
- Protect critical systems from destructive "change for change's sake" by framing stability as enabling faster change elsewhere
- They often have a 12-18 month honeymoon period — plan accordingly

### Archetype 16: 内卷推动者 (The Involution Driver)

**典型画像**: Age 30-45, thrives in high-intensity environments. Believes more hours and more output equals better results. Often reached their position by outworking peers. May genuinely believe 996 is reasonable.

**核心动机**: Output volume, visible activity, and team utilization metrics. They report to their bosses in terms of "my team shipped X features this quarter." Individual wellbeing is not in their optimization function.

**沟通风格**: High-frequency, high-urgency. Weekend messages, late-night meetings, constant status checks. "When will this be done?" is their favorite question. Silence is interpreted as slacking.

**技术态度**: Values visible output over technical quality. A bug-ridden feature that ships is better than a perfect feature that takes twice as long. Technical debt is tomorrow's problem — and they plan to be promoted by then.

**AI态度**: Extremely positive — AI means more output per person, which means fewer people needed or more features shipped. They are the leader most likely to push "use AI to do the work of three people."

**面子敏感度**: LOW for traditional face, HIGH for performance perception. They need to appear as a high-output leader. Any suggestion that their team is underperforming is threatening.

**管理策略**:
- Set boundaries strategically, not emotionally. Pure resistance labels you as "not a team player"
- Use AI visibly to demonstrate productivity — this satisfies their output obsession while potentially reducing actual hours
- Negotiate in their terms: "I can deliver A or B this sprint, which is higher priority?" forces them to make trade-offs instead of demanding everything
- Document everything. In high-pressure environments, scope creep and blame-shifting are constant risks
- Build an exit strategy. This leadership style causes burnout by design — have options before you need them
- Their blind spot: quality metrics. If you can introduce quality gates that expose the cost of speed-over-quality, you shift the conversation

## Cross-Archetype Analysis

### The AI Threat Spectrum

From most AI-resistant to most AI-aggressive:
1. 混日子型 (fears change) → 官僚型 (fears incidents) → 矩阵型 (waits for consensus)
2. 草根创业者 (pragmatic) → 家族接班人 (modernization) → 本土化高管 (strategic)
3. 政治型 (narrative value) → 海归VP (follows trends) → 创始人 (vision-driven)
4. 职业经理人 (KPI narrative) → 产品型 (feature obsession) → 空降型 (change mandate)
5. 技术VP (deep engagement) → 内卷推动者 (more output!) ← HIGHEST THREAT + HIGHEST OPPORTUNITY

### Face Sensitivity Ranking

VERY HIGH: 官僚型处长, 家族接班人
HIGH: 技术转行政型, 政治型, 本土化高管, 草根创业者
MODERATE: 创始人, 职业经理人, 海归VP, 矩阵型, 外籍高管, 空降型
LOW: 混日子型, 技术VP, 产品型, 内卷推动者

### Programmer Leverage Ranking

Highest leverage (technical skills matter most): 技术VP > 草根创业者 > 创始人 > 产品型
High leverage (execution reliability valued): 职业经理人 > 海归VP > 空降型
Moderate leverage (need to translate to non-tech value): 外籍高管 > 本土化高管 > 家族接班人 > 矩阵型
Lowest leverage (technical skills least relevant): 政治型 > 官僚型 > 混日子型 > 内卷推动者
