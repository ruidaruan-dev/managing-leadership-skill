# Scenario Templates (场景策略模板库)

Pre-built strategy templates for the 10 most common workplace scenarios Chinese programmers face. Each template follows a consistent structure: Context Analysis, Stakeholder Map, Risk Assessment, Strategy Options, and Sample Dialogue.

---

## Template Format

Each scenario provides:
- **背景分析**: What is happening and why it matters
- **利益相关者**: Who is involved and what they want
- **风险评估**: What could go wrong
- **策略选项**: Ranked by risk/reward with concrete steps
- **示例对话**: Specific Chinese-language dialogue suggestions

---

## Scenario 1: 领导让你做非技术工作 (Leader Assigns Non-Technical Work)

**背景分析**: Your leader asks you to do tasks outside your technical role — preparing PPTs, organizing events, writing reports, or managing vendor relationships. This is common in SOEs and private enterprises where boundaries are fluid.

**利益相关者**: You (want to focus on technical growth), Leader (needs tasks done by someone reliable), Peers (watching whether you become the "errand person")

**风险评估**:
- Accepting everything: become the permanent "miscellaneous tasks" person, technical skills atrophy
- Refusing everything: labeled as "not a team player," damaged relationship with leader
- The sweet spot: selective acceptance that builds relational capital without derailing your career

**策略选项**:

Option A — Strategic Acceptance (Low Risk):
Accept the task but negotiate the framing: "Happy to help with this. Would it be OK if I also document the process so anyone on the team can handle it next time?" This delivers the favor while creating a system that prevents repeat assignments.

Option B — Redirect with Value (Medium Risk):
"I want to make sure this gets done well. I'm not the best person for PPT design, but I can provide all the technical content and data. Maybe [colleague with design skills] and I can collaborate?" This shows willingness while protecting your time.

Option C — Boundary Setting (Higher Risk, use selectively):
"I'm currently at capacity with [critical project the leader cares about]. If I take this on, [project] will be delayed by X days. Would you like me to reprioritize?" Forces the leader to make the trade-off explicitly.

**示例对话**:
```
场景：处长让你准备一个给上级领导看的数字化转型汇报PPT

回应（策略A）：
"处长好，这个汇报很重要，我一定认真准备。我想确认一下，汇报的核心受众是哪几位领导？
他们最关心哪些方面？我好有针对性地准备技术数据和成果展示。另外我准备整理一套汇报模板，
以后类似的汇报咱们团队任何人都能快速产出，您看可以吗？"
```

## Scenario 2: 领导要用AI替代团队成员 (Leader Wants AI to Replace Team Members)

**背景分析**: The central scenario of the AI era. Your leader asks you to use AI tools to prove that the work of 2-3 colleagues can be automated, setting up potential layoffs. This is a political minefield.

**利益相关者**: Leader (wants to show cost savings to their boss), You (in the crosshairs if you succeed too well), Colleagues (their jobs are at stake), Leader's boss (wants efficiency narrative)

**风险评估**:
- Full compliance: colleagues lose jobs, you are next, and you've proven the playbook works
- Full resistance: labeled as obstructionist, may be first to go
- The paradox: the better you are at AI, the more dangerous and more indispensable you become

**策略选项**:

Option A — The AI Champion Reframe (Recommended):
Enthusiastically adopt AI but reframe from "replacement" to "augmentation": "I've been experimenting and AI can handle about 40% of repetitive testing work. But here's what's interesting — it frees the team to focus on complex scenarios that AI misses. Net result: better quality AND higher throughput. I can lead this transformation."

Key moves:
1. Demonstrate AI capability (shows compliance)
2. Reveal AI limitations (shows expertise they need)
3. Propose augmentation over replacement (protects colleagues while delivering value)
4. Position yourself as the irreplaceable AI-human bridge

Option B — The Data-Driven Delay (Medium Risk):
"Great idea. Let me run a proper evaluation first — I want to make sure we don't introduce quality risks. I'll need 4-6 weeks to test AI tools against our actual workload and produce a credible report." This buys time and positions the eventual report as objective rather than political.

Option C — The Scope Expansion (Strategic):
"If we integrate AI into the team's workflow, we can handle 3x the current workload. Instead of reducing headcount, we could take on [project that leader's boss wants] without additional hiring. That's a much stronger story for leadership." Redirect from cost-cutting to growth narrative.

**示例对话**:
```
场景：技术VP让你评估用AI工具替代三个测试工程师

回应（策略A）：
"VP好，这个方向我非常认同，我最近也一直在研究AI在测试领域的应用。根据我的初步实验，
AI确实能自动化大约40%的常规回归测试。但我发现一个有意思的点：AI处理不了的那60%恰恰
是最容易出生产事故的复杂场景。

我的建议是：与其裁减人员，不如让AI接管重复性工作，让测试团队专攻复杂场景和AI测试用例
的维护。这样我们的测试覆盖率能从目前的65%提升到90%以上，同时团队规模不变但产出翻倍。

这个故事在向上汇报时也更有说服力——'我们用AI让团队效率翻倍'比'我们裁了三个人'
更能展示技术领导力。

如果您同意，我可以用两周时间做一个完整的POC，拿数据来说话。"
```

## Scenario 3: 汇报项目延期 (Reporting Project Delays)

**背景分析**: The project will miss its deadline. How you deliver this news determines whether you are seen as a responsible professional or a failure.

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Early Warning (Best Practice):
Deliver the news as early as possible, with a recovery plan:
1. State the situation factually (no excuses, no blame)
2. Explain the root cause in business terms
3. Present a recovery plan with revised timeline
4. Offer trade-offs (scope reduction to hit original date, or full scope with new date)

Option B — The Pre-emptive Reframe:
Before the delay is obvious, proactively propose scope adjustment: "Based on our progress, I recommend we split into Phase 1 (core features, on time) and Phase 2 (remaining features, 2 weeks later). This ensures we deliver value on schedule."

**示例对话**:
```
场景：向民企老板汇报项目将延期两周

回应（策略A）：
"老板，关于XX项目我需要跟您同步一个情况。目前核心功能开发进度正常，但第三方支付接口的
对接比预期复杂——他们的API文档和实际接口有出入，我们需要额外两周来完成联调和测试。

我的建议是两个方案供您选择：
方案一：按原定时间上线核心功能，支付功能两周后通过热更新补上；
方案二：整体延期两周，一次性完整上线。

两个方案的技术风险我都评估过了。方案一上线快但后续有热更新风险，方案二更稳但晚两周。
您倾向哪个方向？"
```

## Scenario 4: 争取资源/HC (Requesting Resources or Headcount)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Business Case Approach:
Frame resources as investment, not cost: "Adding one senior engineer will accelerate [leader's priority project] delivery by 6 weeks. The market window closes in Q3."

Option B — The Risk Mitigation Approach:
"Currently our bus factor on [critical system] is 1 — if I get hit by a bus, no one can maintain it. A second engineer provides business continuity." (Especially effective with risk-averse leaders.)

**示例对话**:
```
场景：向国企处长申请增加一个编制

回应：
"处长，我想跟您汇报一下目前团队的工作负荷情况。目前我们三个人同时支撑着A、B、C三个系统，
日常运维就占了60%的精力，新需求排队已经到了下季度。

上周B系统出了一次小故障，幸好及时发现了。但说实话，以目前的人力配置，类似风险会越来越高。

如果能增加一个人，我的计划是让新人专门负责B系统运维，这样现有团队可以集中精力保障
A系统的升级——这也是上面一直在催的项目。您看这个方案可行吗？需要我准备一份详细的
岗位需求和人力规划给您审批？"
```

## Scenario 5: 薪资/晋升谈判 (Salary and Promotion Negotiation)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Achievement Anchor:
Time the conversation after a major win. Lead with value delivered, then state your request.

Option B — The Market Reference:
"I recently got some recruiter outreach. I'm not looking — I enjoy working here. But it made me realize my current compensation may be below market for my role and contributions. Can we discuss?"

**示例对话**:
```
场景：在大厂向直属leader提晋升

回应：
"leader，我想找个时间跟你聊聊我的职业发展。过去一年我主导了XX系统重构，系统可用性从
99.5%提到99.95%，也带了两个新人完成了独立交付。

我对照了公司的晋升标准，我认为在技术深度、项目影响力和团队贡献三个维度上，
我已经达到了下一级的要求。

我想请教一下，从你的角度看，我还有哪些方面需要加强？下一个晋升窗口期，
你觉得我应该怎么准备？如果你认可的话，我希望能得到你的提名支持。"
```

## Scenario 6: 领导抢功 (Leader Takes Credit for Your Work)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Strategic Acceptance:
Let the leader take credit publicly but ensure key people know the truth through informal channels. "I heard Leader gave a great presentation about the project. I was happy to provide all the technical data and implementation — glad it landed well."

Option B — The Paper Trail:
Create visible artifacts: send emails summarizing your contributions, write technical blog posts, present at team-wide tech talks. These create a record that persists beyond any single meeting.

## Scenario 7: 多线领导之间周旋 (Navigating Between Conflicting Leaders)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Transparency Bridge:
"I want to make sure I'm aligned with both you and [other leader]. Your priorities are X, their priorities are Y. Here's my proposed allocation of effort — does this work for you?" Surface the conflict without choosing sides.

Option B — The Escalation Trigger:
When conflicts are irreconcilable: "I'm getting conflicting direction on priorities. I don't want to make the wrong call. Could the three of us align on this?" This forces resolution at the leadership level where it belongs.

## Scenario 8: 新领导到任建立关系 (New Leader Arrives)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Proactive Brief:
Request an early 1-on-1: "I'd like to give you a technical overview of our systems and current projects. I want to make sure you have the context you need to hit the ground running."

Key principles:
- Provide useful information without being political about predecessors
- Demonstrate competence and willingness to support
- Ask about their priorities and preferred working style
- Do NOT complain about the previous leader or organizational problems (yet)

---

## Scenario 9: 绩效评审准备 (Performance Review Preparation)

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Pre-Review Campaign:
Start 4-6 weeks before the review period:
1. Compile your achievements with metrics and business impact
2. Collect positive feedback from stakeholders (ask via email so it is documented)
3. Have an informal pre-review chat with your leader: "I want to make sure we're aligned on how this year went"
4. Prepare counterarguments for likely criticisms
5. Come with a growth plan for next year (shows forward-thinking)

## Scenario 10: 被要求评价同事 (Asked to Evaluate Colleagues)

**背景分析**: Your leader asks for your honest assessment of a colleague. This is a trust test AND a political trap.

**策略选项**:

Option A — The Balanced Diplomat:
Provide honest assessment framed constructively: "Zhang is very strong technically, especially in [specific area]. The area I think could accelerate their growth is [specific, actionable feedback]. We complement each other well on the team."

Key principles:
- Never trash a colleague (even if deserved). It damages your reputation more than theirs.
- Be specific, not vague. "Good person" is useless. "Strong in X, growing in Y" is useful.
- If the colleague is genuinely problematic, focus on behavioral impact, not character judgments.
- Consider what the leader is really asking — they may already have an opinion and want confirmation.
